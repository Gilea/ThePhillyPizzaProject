About Us

Three friends. One city. Zero bias.

The Project

The Philly Pizza Project started as a simple idea: Philadelphia has a serious pizza scene, and nobody was documenting it with the rigor it deserves. We wanted to change that.

We visit restaurants together — always as a group of three — so every score you see on this site reflects a genuine collective judgment, not a single palate. We order multiple pizzas per visit, score each one independently, then average our results into a final group score that goes into the ranking.

We have three separate, never-combined rankings: Neapolitan, New York Style, and Detroit Style. We believe these are fundamentally different products that deserve to be evaluated on their own terms.

How We Score

Every pizza is scored on five criteria. Every restaurant visit is scored on four additional criteria. All scores are weighted — not just averaged — because not all criteria are equally important.

Pizza Criteria

CriterionWeightWhat we look for
Dough25%Texture, fermentation, structure. The foundation of everything.
Sauce20%Tomato quality, balance, intensity. Does it taste like real tomatoes?
Cheese20%Quality, distribution, melt. Is it the right cheese for the style?
Bake15%Bottom char, color, heat spots. Is it properly cooked?
Overall Balance20%Ingredient ratio, first bite cohesion. Does it all work together?

Restaurant Criteria

CriterionWeightWhat we look for
Service25%Friendliness, speed, attention to detail.
Atmosphere25%Space, sound levels, overall vibe.
Value30%Price relative to quality. The most important restaurant criterion.
Consistency20%Menu coherence, reliable execution.

The Final Score

The score that goes into the ranking is the group average of Guillem, Matt, and Montse's overall impression scores. Each of us gives a holistic score for the visit — not a formula, just an honest number that captures how we felt about the experience overall. The criteria tables exist to inform that judgment and to give readers a transparent breakdown.

Occasional guest reviewers join us and their scores appear in the write-up, but they don't affect the ranking average.
